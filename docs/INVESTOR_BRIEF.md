# Ludex -- Investor Brief

**Confidential | March 2026**

---

## A. ONE-LINER

Ludex is a play-to-learn RPG where 200 million Latin American youth master financial literacy through AI-powered gameplay and earn on-chain credentials that prove it.

---

## B. PROBLEM (With Data)

Latin America faces a financial literacy catastrophe among its youngest and largest demographic:

| Pain Point | Data |
|---|---|
| Youth without meaningful financial education | **200 million** across LATAM under age 25 (ECLAC, 2024) |
| LATAM financial literacy rate (adults) | **35%** -- vs. 57% OECD average (S&P Global FinLit Survey) |
| Youth (15-24) with a bank account in LATAM | **26%** (World Bank Findex, 2024) |
| Informal debt prevalence among LATAM youth | **45%** borrow from informal sources at 100-400% APR (IDB, 2023) |
| Time LATAM youth spend gaming daily | **3.2 hours** average (Newzoo, 2024) |
| Dropout rate from traditional financial education programs | **78%** within first month (CAF Development Bank, 2023) |
| GDP cost of financial illiteracy in LATAM | **$50 billion+** annually in predatory lending, fraud, and suboptimal financial decisions |

**The paradox:** LATAM youth spend 3+ hours daily in games, yet financial education is delivered through PDFs, classrooms, and apps with 78% churn. The delivery mechanism is completely mismatched with the audience.

---

## C. SOLUTION

Ludex is a **GameFi RPG** built on OneChain where every quest teaches a real financial concept, every NPC is an AI tutor (powered by Claude), and every achievement is a verifiable on-chain credential.

**10x improvements:**

| Dimension | Traditional FinEd | Duolingo-style Apps | Ludex |
|---|---|---|---|
| Engagement model | Classroom/PDF | Gamified quizzes | **Full RPG world** |
| Personalization | None | Difficulty scaling | **AI NPCs adapt to each player** |
| Retention (30-day) | 22% | 35% | **65%+ (projected, based on RPG engagement data)** |
| Incentive | Certificate | Streaks | **On-chain tokens + verifiable credentials** |
| Financial concepts taught | Theory | Basic vocabulary | **Applied decision-making through simulation** |
| Credential portability | PDF certificate | Badge in app | **Soulbound token on-chain -- portable, verifiable** |
| Cost to learner | $50-500 course fee | Free/premium | **Free-to-play, earn rewards** |

**Core mechanics:**
- **RPG city with districts** -- each district maps to a financial domain (Market District = supply/demand; Bank of Ludex = compound interest; Insurance Guild = risk management; Portfolio Arena = diversification)
- **Claude-powered AI NPCs** -- every NPC adapts conversation complexity to the player's demonstrated knowledge level; no two players get the same dialogue
- **On-chain credentials (Soulbound)** -- completed modules mint verifiable credentials on OneChain that can be shared with employers, universities, and financial institutions
- **Community treasury** -- top performers earn token rewards funded by ecosystem partnerships and protocol fees

## D. WHY NOW

1. **AI NPC breakthrough**: Claude and GPT-4 class models finally enable NPCs that can hold coherent, adaptive, pedagogically sound conversations. This was impossible 2 years ago. AI NPCs are the unlock that makes "learn through story" work at scale.

2. **OneChain and Move language**: Move-based blockchains offer resource-oriented programming ideal for game assets and credentials. OneChain's focus on gaming/social use cases provides infrastructure, grants, and ecosystem support.

3. **GameFi recovery**: The 2021-2022 GameFi wave (Axie Infinity, StepN) proved demand but failed on gameplay quality. The market has reset expectations -- "play-to-earn" is dead, "play-to-learn" with real utility is the next wave.

4. **LATAM mobile gaming explosion**: LATAM is the fastest-growing mobile gaming market globally (+12% YoY), with 300M+ gamers. Brazil and Mexico are top-10 global gaming markets by revenue.

5. **Institutional demand for financial literacy**: The IDB, CAF, and World Bank have collectively committed $2B+ to financial inclusion programs in LATAM. They are actively seeking scalable, digital-first delivery mechanisms.

6. **Credential verification need**: LATAM employers and neobanks increasingly want proof of financial competency. Soulbound tokens on-chain provide tamper-proof, portable verification.

## M. RISKS AND MITIGATIONS

| # | Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Engagement plateau** -- players complete available content and churn before new districts launch | High | Procedurally generated side quests via Claude; seasonal events; PvP (Portfolio Arena) provides infinite replayability; community-created challenges; planned 12-month content roadmap pre-launch |
| 2 | **AI NPC quality / cost** -- Claude API costs spike with scale or response quality degrades | High | Prompt caching reduces per-conversation cost by 60-80%; fine-tuned smaller models for common interactions; hybrid approach (scripted + AI); negotiate volume pricing with Anthropic as education partner |
| 3 | **Regulatory risk around GameFi / tokens** -- LATAM regulators classify game tokens as securities | Medium | Soulbound credentials are non-transferable (not securities); game rewards are utility tokens for in-game use; legal opinion from securities counsel in each jurisdiction; separate credential system from reward system |
| 4 | **School adoption friction** -- institutional sales cycles are slow (6-12 months) | Medium | Product-led growth through direct-to-student adoption creates bottom-up demand; free tier for schools removes budget objection; grant funding (IDB, CAF) covers institutional pilots; start with private schools (faster decision-making) |
| 5 | **Game quality vs. competition** -- AAA mobile games set high expectations | Medium | Focus on narrative quality and AI NPC depth rather than graphics; 2.5D art style is achievable with small team; RPG genre is about story and systems, not visual fidelity; Stardew Valley proved indie RPGs can win on charm |
